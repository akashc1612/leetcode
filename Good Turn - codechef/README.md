# Good Turn
## Easy
<div id="problem-statement" class="_problemBody_15v7d_29 print"><h2>Problem</h2>
<p>Chef and Chefina are playing with dice. In one turn, both of them roll their dice at once.</p>
<p>They consider a turn to be <em>good</em> if the <strong>sum</strong> of the numbers on their dice is greater than <span class="math math-inline"><span class="katex"><span class="katex-mathml"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>6</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">6</annotation></semantics></math></span><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height: 0.6444em;"></span><span class="mord">6</span></span></span></span></span>.<br>
Given that in a particular turn Chef and Chefina got <span class="math math-inline"><span class="katex"><span class="katex-mathml"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>X</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">X</annotation></semantics></math></span><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height: 0.6833em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right: 0.07847em;">X</span></span></span></span></span> and <span class="math math-inline"><span class="katex"><span class="katex-mathml"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>Y</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">Y</annotation></semantics></math></span><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height: 0.6833em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right: 0.22222em;">Y</span></span></span></span></span> on their respective dice, find whether the turn was good.</p>
<h3>Input Format</h3>
<ul>
<li>The first line of input will contain a single integer <span class="math math-inline"><span class="katex"><span class="katex-mathml"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>T</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">T</annotation></semantics></math></span><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height: 0.6833em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right: 0.13889em;">T</span></span></span></span></span>, denoting the number of test cases.</li>
<li>Each test case contains two space-separated integers <span class="math math-inline"><span class="katex"><span class="katex-mathml"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>X</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">X</annotation></semantics></math></span><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height: 0.6833em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right: 0.07847em;">X</span></span></span></span></span> and <span class="math math-inline"><span class="katex"><span class="katex-mathml"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>Y</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">Y</annotation></semantics></math></span><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height: 0.6833em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right: 0.22222em;">Y</span></span></span></span></span> — the numbers Chef and Chefina got on their respective dice.</li>
</ul>
<h3>Output Format</h3>
<p>For each test case, output on a new line, <code>YES</code>, if the turn was good and <code>NO</code> otherwise.</p>
<p>Each character of the output may be printed in either uppercase or lowercase. That is, the strings <code>NO</code>, <code>no</code>, <code>nO</code>, and <code>No</code> will be treated as equivalent.</p>
